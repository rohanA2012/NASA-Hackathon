Coltsfoot

Bloom Time: February through April, sometimes extending to June in some regions

Habitat: disturbed, open, low-lying areas like roadsides, stream banks, pastures, and waste ground, and is widespread in its native Eurasia and North Africa, as well as in North America

Description: a perennial herb known for its "son before father" growth habit, with its dandelion-like yellow flowers appearing in early spring before its distinct, hoof-shaped leaves emerge.
